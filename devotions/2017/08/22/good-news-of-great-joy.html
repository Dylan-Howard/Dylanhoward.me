Good News of Great Joy

devotion

Aug 22, 2017

Dylan Howard

"In the same region, shepherds were staying out in the fields and keeping watch at night over their flock. Then an angel of the Lord stood before them, and the glory of the Lord shone around them, and they were terrified. But the angel said to them, "Don’t be afraid, for look, I proclaim to you good news of great joy that will be for all the people: Today a Savior, who is Messiah the Lord, was born for you in the city of David."

Luke 2:8-11

"...I proclaim to you good news of great joy..." – Luke 2:10

Lately, I’ve been so gripped by this section in the story of Jesus’s birth. Take a closer look at what the angel tells the shepherds.

Indeed, this is true, the angel was telling them the greatest of news, which inspires the greatest of joy. Consider the vastness of our sin and the extent of our evil. All too often we sink into the vain pursuits of our flesh. In the midst of this, the Holy One has come to us and not only drank the cup of God’s wrath-that we fully deserved—but He also drank the same bitter cup of sin we loved. The shame we carried, the guilt we carried, but most important of all, the sin we carried, was transferred into one God-man.

Indeed, the joy of proclaimed to the shepherd in Luke—that is, the pleasure of Christ completing our salvation—is now also our joy. Our sin is gone, and, in its place, is deep and abounding joy. This joy is no longer simply a message, but an actual outgrowth of the Spirit living in followers of Christ.

As Hebrews puts it,

"...Jesus... who for the joy that was set before him endured the cross, despising the shame..." Hebrews 12:2 [Abridged]

Understand, this joy is not necessarily followed by an outburst of emotion, but it is followed by a sense of peace and calm, comfort and warmth, and an intense understanding of God’s beauty, immensity, and continual goodness. This joy—which perseveres all circumstances—is rooted in the promise of God working all things to end in His glory and our joy in Him.

O LORD,

all of your ways end in the delight of all of Your people. You wept and suffered so that we may rejoice, and, now, we are unworthy recipients of Your grace. How great a joy we have! Let us rest in the thought of Your love, pardon for sin, and our future sinless state.

We often belittle Your blood and slight Your love, but can, in repentance, return to You—our endless fountain of grace and joy. Let us dwell on this great news and delight in its comfort and peace. May we always be in awe of this and seek to share it. To Christ be the glory, amen.
